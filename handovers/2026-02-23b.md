## Expert panel review — full detail

### The imaginary room
Huberman · Peter Attia · Rhonda Patrick · Inigo San Millán · Matthew Walker (sleep scientist) · Tim Ferriss · behavioral economist · CTO (health platform experience) · longevity GP

### What they said about the current format

**Tim Ferriss:** "After reading this email, what does Matthew actually do differently on Monday morning? If you can't answer that in one sentence, the format is failing its primary job." The digest is excellent as a data summary; weak as a behavior-change instrument. No explicit call to action. Insight of the Week helps but was buried.

**The CTO:** "Where's the score? Where's the single number at the top that tells me in 2 seconds whether this was a good week or not?" — Every section required reading to understand direction. A good dashboard shows the verdict before the evidence. → Fixed in v3 with scorecard header.

**Peter Attia:** "You're measuring outputs — weight, HRV, sleep score, miles. Those are lagging indicators. The platform has almost no leading indicators. You don't have blood work. You don't have glucose. You don't have subjective data. You have the exhaust, not the engine." Biggest structural gap: without mood/energy/stress/biomarkers the data describes what happened but can't explain why.

**Matthew Walker (sleep scientist):** "Sleep score is a black box. What drove it? Sleep efficiency? WASO? REM? Duration? These aren't the same thing and they don't respond to the same interventions." Would add: sleep efficiency target ≥85%, WASO (wake after sleep onset), REM% (target 20-25%), deep sleep% (target 15-20%), social jetlag, sleep debt as a rolling number. → Fixed in v3 with sleep architecture section.

**Huberman on timing:** "A weekly email is not a feedback loop — it's a postmortem. By Sunday morning you've already made 168 hours of decisions without this data informing them. The highest-leverage format is a daily brief that takes 30 seconds to read. One number. One action. No analysis." Weekly is right for reflection/pattern recognition. But it shouldn't be the only cadence. → Daily brief is the #1 next build (see below).

**Inigo San Millán on Zone 2:** "Average heart rate is a very coarse proxy. If Matthew does a 90-minute walk at 115bpm and a 45-minute run where he spends 20 minutes at 125 and 20 at 145, the averages look similar but the metabolic training effect is completely different. You also need Zone 2 as a percentage of total training volume, not just raw minutes. 30 Zone 2 minutes out of 400 total cardio is a different picture than 90/120." → Partially fixed in v3 with Zone 2%. True HR time-series analysis remains future work.

**Rhonda Patrick:** "Matthew is in Seattle. Pacific Northwest. He is almost certainly vitamin D deficient October through May and the platform has mock nutrition data. Until MacroFactor is real, the nutrition section is theater. And even with real data, you're only tracking macros. Omega-3 index, magnesium, zinc — these directly affect HRV, sleep architecture, and recovery." → MacroFactor real data remains the highest-priority unlock. Micronutrient tools exist in v2.3.0 but are blocked on real data.

**Behavioral economist (most important voice):** "The platform is very good at telling Matthew what happened. It's completely silent on why he didn't do the things he already knows he should do. The gap between knowing and doing isn't a data problem — it's a friction and motivation problem. The most valuable section of this email doesn't exist yet: a section that asks 'what got in the way this week?' and over time builds a model of Matthew's personal friction patterns." This insight underpins the Coach Maya Rodriguez persona added in v3.

### What the panel said about cadence

One cadence is wrong. Three are needed:

**Daily brief** — the panel's #1 priority (was backlog #16). Huberman: "A weekly email is a postmortem. The highest-leverage format is a daily brief that takes 30 seconds to read." Pushed by 8:15am PT (after all ingests). One readiness number + color. One sentence. One action. Could be iMessage or email. No analysis. Goal: influence the *next* 24 hours, not reflect on the last 7 days.

**Monthly coach's letter** — first Sunday of each month. Not a 4-week rollup — a narrative. Haiku writes a "coach's letter" covering: 30-day trend direction per pillar, ahead/behind on annual goals, one thing clearly working, one thing clearly not, recommended focus for next 30 days.

**Quarterly deep-dive** — ideally timed with blood work. Longitudinal progress since journey start. Body composition trajectory. Fitness age vs calendar age. Goal projection: at current trajectory, when does Matthew hit his weight goal, what will VO2 max estimate be, what's predicted RHR at 40? The document you'd bring to a doctor's appointment.


```
[Large header card]
```

**Logic:**
- Call `get_readiness_score` equivalent inline (don't hit MCP — query DynamoDB directly for yesterday's Whoop + Eight Sleep + 7d HRV + TSB)
- Color thresholds: GREEN ≥80, YELLOW 60-79, RED <60
- Train guidance: GREEN = "Hard session OK" / YELLOW = "Moderate — avoid high strain" / RED = "Rest or easy only"
- Caffeine cutoff: simple rule first pass — if HRV trend is declining, push cutoff earlier (1pm vs 2pm). Later: use actual MacroFactor caffeine-sleep correlation once #2 from backlog is built.
- Sleep target: countdown from wake time minus 7.5hrs
- Haiku call: optional, max_tokens=200, single-sentence insight referencing actual yesterday numbers. Fallback gracefully to no insight if Haiku call fails.
- Entire email should render in < 200 lines of HTML

**IAM:** Same `lambda-weekly-digest-role` works (already has DynamoDB read, Secrets Manager, SES)
**New EventBridge rule:** `daily-brief-schedule` on `weekly-digest` function (or new Lambda — new function preferred for clean separation)
