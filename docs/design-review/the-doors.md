Wall"** where I talk back to the AI.

## STAGE 2 — The review (after assembling + introducing the panel)
Panel reacts in their own voices, disagreeing where they would, and delivers:
1. **The whole-site story** — does it read as ONE coherent documentary, or disjointed pages? Where

   newcomer in 10 seconds, *without* rebalancing away from me-first?
2. **Per-door uplevels** — for each door, the single highest-impact visual/interaction/editorial
   move (concrete; name the element). Protect the ownable signatures; extend them.
3. **The "week one" frame** — how to make the experiment's youth the most compelling, least-braggy
   hook across the doors (it's the truest, most shareable thing).
4. **The moat features** — how to make the named-coach board + the Third Wall (AI↔me dialogue) feel
   like the soul of the site, not a feature. (Note: the Third Wall is currently one-sided — I
   haven't replied yet; the panel should weigh how much that matters.)
5. **What to cut / merge** — door-count, duplicated dispatches readers, vanity surfaces.
6. **First-10-seconds** — what a stranger sees on Home, tastefully, without betraying me-first.
Rank by impact. Honor the design system + honesty + me-first + the locked 5-door model. Mark
**buildable now** vs **needs more weeks of data/story** (it's week one — there isn't much elapsed
arc yet, and the design must be honest about that).
