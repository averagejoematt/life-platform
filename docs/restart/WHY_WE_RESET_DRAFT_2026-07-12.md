On June 24th I logged my last meal. June 25th, my last workout. June 26th, my last
weigh-in. June 27th, the last habit check. Then — nothing. For two weeks the only
signals coming out of my life were the ones I didn't have to lift a finger for: the
strap on my wrist, the mattress under my back.

Here's the uncomfortable part. If you visited the site during those two weeks, most of
it looked fine. Recovery scores in the 80s. Sleep charts filling in every night. A
training page still bragging about "the volume ramp." My AI coaches — the ones whose
whole job is to read me — kept finding things to praise, because the numbers they could
see genuinely looked good. Rest inflates recovery. Absence reads as calm. My character
sheet — the RPG-style scorecard of my life — actually **leveled up five times** while I
was gone, because of a bug in how it treated days with no evidence.

The wearables kept talking. I went silent. And the machine couldn't tell the difference
between a man who was fine and a man who had checked out.

Some parts of the system did notice — the presence detector flagged the silence, one
coach flatly said she wouldn't invent a story about why I'd gone quiet, and the
chronicle wrote that "the person inside the body left no testimony." But nothing forced
the rest of the platform to care. That's the failure worth writing down: not that I
stopped logging — humans lapse, that's the least surprising finding in behavior science
— but that a platform built on "honest numbers" could narrate two dark weeks as a
gentle dip.

So we reset. Sunday, July 12th, is Day 0 of cycle 5. New baseline weight from Sunday
morning's weigh-in. Counters back to zero. The old data isn't deleted — every prior
cycle is archived and browsable, this stall included. It's a chapter, not a deleted
scene.

A reset without a diff is just a new countdown, so here's the diff — all shipped before
genesis, all verifiable in the repo:

1. **Silence is now a first-class signal.** Every manual source has a declared dropout
   threshold. Go dark and a severity ladder (quiet → loud → alarm) is injected into
   every AI surface — coaches, chronicle, podcast, daily brief. At alarm level, a coach
   who fails to address the gap in their opening paragraph gets their output rejected
   by a deterministic gate. Sounding cheerful over a dead log is no longer possible.
2. **The character detrains.** No level-ups on days with no evidence (that bug is
   fixed and regression-tested), stat atrophy after three dark days, visible XP debt,
   and a dormant visual state. Neglect the inputs, watch the avatar fade — like every
   game you've ever played.
3. **Dead sources look dead.** No more "301 LBS TODAY" off a two-week-old weigh-in, no
   more "Rest day" on day 15 of a layoff. Every number now carries its as-of date, and
   charts stop drawing trends they can't support.
4. **The coaches can now ask me questions.** A new check-in loop lets them ask what the
   sensors can't see — what got in the way, what feels heavy — and my answers become
   context they reason with. Skipping a question is always allowed; the point is
   context, not interrogation.
5. **The comeback is pre-registered.** Week one runs at roughly half my previous
   training volume, no max attempts before day 21, baselines re-earned over 14 days
   (weight is a 7-day rolling average, not a single hopeful morning). The coaches are
   bound to those caps — a fresh 85% recovery score is not a license to be a hero on
   day one.

The rule for next time is also written down now: a lapse triggers questions by day
four, an honest "manual signal dark" state by day seven, and a re-entry menu by day
ten. Most future stumbles should become chapters inside a cycle — not new cycles. If I
ever reset again, you should expect this same document: what broke, what changed.

Day 0 is Sunday. The scale gets the first word.
