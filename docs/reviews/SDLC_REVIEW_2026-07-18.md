## Executive summary

The SDLC is unusually strong where the platform has pointed its signature weapon — gates that
make drift structural — and weak exactly where it hasn't: **the gates that guard the product
do not guard the process.** Three meta-classes account for most of the 60 confirmed findings:

1. **Dead controls documents claim are alive.** The production manual-approval gate does not
   exist on the GitHub `production` environment (deploys start 4–5s after Plan, zero approvals
   back to mid-June) while ADR-065/ADR-117/CLAUDE.md/ci-cd.yml all state it does — and the
   remediation agent's "auto-merge does NOT auto-deploy" safety case rests on it. Same class:
   no branch protection at all, a PR template 0/20 recent PRs use, issue templates never used
   (still quoting the superseded $75 ceiling), deploy/README.md teaching a boot-broken MCP zip
   procedure, a Makefile with six dead targets, and an ADR index that silently omits 12 ADRs
   while its `--check` self-certifies current.
2. **Alert→action loops that don't close.** `life-platform/ai-keys` is 132 days old against a
   90-day written SLA with its staleness alert firing daily for 12+ days, unactioned. Main-branch
   CI is normalized-red (19 of the last 100 runs green; the handover declares "main GREEN" on a
   sha whose run failed) because two known flake classes recur as memory rituals instead of
   structural fixes. The public serving path throttled real readers 627 times in 30 days at a
   daily-saturating concurrency cap of 5 — with no Throttles alarm anywhere. Auto-rollbacks
   fire and appear in no log a human reads.
3. **Written contract vs practiced mechanics.** ADR-099's scoring formula is a dead letter on
   the dominant filing path (transparent, but unamended); the release topology (no staging,
   single account, detect-and-revert) was **REFUTED as a defect and CONFIRMED as an accident** —
   the posture is defensible for this platform, and nothing writes it down; subscriber-email
   retention — the only third-party PII held — is decided by a fictional AI persona's code
   comment, contradicting the canonical governance doc, and the delete lambda structurally
   cannot delete one subscriber.

Honesty requires the inverse list too. The panel **confirmed correct** (per the review bar):
backlog/milestone hygiene most funded teams don't have, the reconcile-bot design, structural
subagent-verification discipline, the no-load-testing posture (traffic doesn't justify it —
write it down), the memory-backup leg (live, today's sync verified in S3), pip-audit existing
(the "no SCA" pre-seed hypothesis was FALSE as stated — the real gaps are narrower: binary
layers unscanned, Dependabot *alerts* disabled, secret scanning lost on going private).

**Grades: A- ideation · A- AI-practice · B+ planning · B+ deploy · B+ ops · B+ knowledge ·
