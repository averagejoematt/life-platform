## Executive synthesis — eight themes across 83 findings

1. **The engagement machine is elite and empty.** Predict-the-week, convene-the-board, since-last-visit, the character sheet, serial recaps — all live, all well-made, all playing to ~1 confirmed subscriber. Meanwhile the acquisition plumbing is broken at every stage: the RSS feed rebuilds daily from a dead pre-reset posts file (newest item May 3), chronicle posts are invisible to search (absent from the sitemap, hub is an uncrawlable JS shell), the generated post pages still use the v3 template with no covers/canonical/subscribe CTA, and the day-2 welcome email has served its fallback since launch. **Distribution before monetization; pipes before features.**
2. **The flagship reader-facing AI is publicly broken.** `/api/board_ask` answers from a phantom persona cast that is not the site's roster, ungrounded ("I don't have any information about Matthew"), with a stale fallback key that 500s, while the *internal* coach AI is deeply grounded (stances, canonical facts, self-correction). One cast, one grounding path — route reader questions to the real 8-coach roster reading `STANCE#latest`.
3. **The platform measures Matthew brilliantly and itself poorly.** Traffic dark since Jun 29; the AI-cost self-metric undercounts real Bedrock billing ~3×; the wedge-A monetization gate (30 lb + sustained list) is instrumented nowhere; the subscriber metric is polluted by never-cleaned canary records (whose tombstone has silently failed since launch — and the simplest fix, delete, is already IAM-permitted).
4. **Config drift is the systemic infra class.** The CloudFront-logging revert is the proven instance; the authoritative DynamoDB table, both GitHub-OIDC roles, SES config, DNS, and several SSM params all live outside IaC with no baseline or drift check. A weekly drift sentinel + a "managed-where" ledger closes the class.
5. **Doc-truth wears a fresh façade.** The auto-sync stamps today's date on bodies asserting layer v76 (live: v93); it launders a March fallback "9 secrets" daily (live: 21); CLAUDE.md is 104KB of which 84% is a 26-entry session diary with stale self-labeled source-of-truth counts; BACKLOG.md was ~27% wrong. The structural fix: promote the sync to a **drift gate** (`--check` asserting body literals against discovered values) and put the diary where it belongs (handovers/).
6. **Release integrity lags the guardrail engineering.** Main has been red in roughly two-thirds of recent runs on locally-runnable gates; the site — the actual product — deploys by hand outside CI (the root of the squash-drift class); there is no automated main==live check despite /version.json existing precisely for that. The clobber guards are excellent; the pipeline around them needs to catch up.
7. **Cost is honest but the ceiling is mis-shaped.** June's breach was dev-session AI re-runs, not production. ~$35/mo is fixed overhead at zero readers; the budget defense de-funds the reader-facing ask endpoints first — backwards for growth. Levers: batch API for non-interactive content AI (~50% off the largest bucket), a CloudWatch alarm/metric audit (~$5/mo), dev-vs-prod spend attribution, and an explicit decision about what the ceiling protects.
8. **Subtract more than add.** 105 of 136 MCP tools unused in 30 days; the remediation agent runs in `auto` yet has produced zero PRs in a month (its last run burned its budget and emitted a broken report); a 64-entry frozen orphan allowlist. The platform over-builds relative to an audience of one — the review's most consistent 185-test-friendly move is deletion.
