## The Timeline (Matthew's words, unfiltered)

### The Long Pattern (2017–2024)
- Weight struggle on and off through most of life
- Biggest consistent rut: 2017–2026
- Has done "big 100lb weight losses before" — this is a recurring cycle

### The Breaking Point (Late 2024)
- Cruise with friends
- Anxiety attacks at last port (Victoria, BC)
- Was hiding the attacks from friends
- Internal realization: "this cannot go on. These ups and downs of years must stop."

### The Victory (Late 2024 – Spring 2025)
- "Ruthless" — different from previous attempts
- 36 consecutive weeks of daily stretching
- Progression: walking → rucking Washington mountains with 25lb+ ruck bags
- Saturday 10-mile runs followed by workouts
- Clean eating
- Fitting into old clothes again
- "In great shape, and felt much better"
- Self-described as: "I crushed it"

### The Unraveling (Spring – Fall 2025)
- **Spring 2025**: Sister-in-law diagnosed with cancer again ("sounding worse this time")
- **Summer 2025**: Sister-in-law passed away from cancer
  - This was the second cancer death in family (mother died 2019)
- **Work**: Layoff rumors and uncertainty — for both his own role and his team
  - Rumors persisted spring → actioned in October
  - Dealing with fallout afterward
- "For whatever reason" — disruption to routine, impact of another cancer loss, guilt/nerves around layoffs — "everything went backwards"

### The Slide (Fall 2025 – Early 2026)
- Traveled to UK — started well with routine, eventually slid
- Hiked Machu Picchu — routine had started slipping by then, but "managed the mountain with ease, felt great doing so"
- Return to Seattle: "the Door Dash orders started, the gym routine failed, everything got thrown out with it"
- Emotional state: "not clinically depressed" but felt "depressed, sad, lonely, and just coping, yet masking through all of work"
- Started questioning relationship more openly with partner
- "More existential thoughts"
- Brief routine attempts: "lasted a couple of days instead of months"
- "This was the norm until the new year"

### The Isolation (Early 2026)
- New Year didn't produce the usual fresh-start results
- End of March — "still not there"
- Started cancelling plans:
  - Cancelled best friend from high school's wedding (cited health/anxiety)
  - Cancelled Hawaii trip with girlfriend and her family (same reasons)
  - "Wanting to isolate"
- Embarrassment: "how great everyone seen me accomplish and conquer this just 6 months ago, only to have put 100lb of weight right back on"
- "I let myself down so much, I made so many promises and commitments this was the one and never again"
- Threw out all large clothes → had to buy entire wardrobe again
- "I barely leave the house these days"

### The Insight
- "This is not just weight loss"
- "The binge eating is not because I love food and am always hungry"
- Somewhere along the road — mother's death, work stress, politics — "whether we want to say subtle trauma, I don't know"
- Key realization: "even if I do my entire 100lb weight loss one more time, more bad things in life will be thrown at me — and I need to be able to cope with this"
- "I need to not just Goggins it"
- "I need to be more rounded — I need to think about my mind, my purpose, my social life, what I do for others"
- Started therapy (since paused)
- AI therapy also provided feedback
- "At a certain point I just felt I need to get back on this horse or I may have real serious health issues, or drift away to my late 40s with a ton of regrets from hiding from all the world"

### The Platform Origin
- Practical reality: Senior Director role with demands to build with AI
- Platform born from dual purpose:
  1. Real use case to understand AI tech better → apply to work conversations
  2. Something productive to do at home instead of "Xbox or TV or just being a lazy person"
- "Replacing Xbox or TV" — filling the void with building

### The Existential Layer
- "I have so many reasons to be grateful — my life, my family, what I have accomplished, what safety nets I have built"
- "Yet I feel still an extra in my own life now. I used to be a main character."
- Worried he's "doing all of this in temporary setting, ticking boxes that don't exist"
- Questioning: career, relationship, where he lives, who his friends are, "if I even like myself, who I am"
- AI feedback: "you over-intellectualize problems as a coping mechanism"
- His response: "maybe I just play the card I have and try to use it as a strength"
- Build the platform → "intellectualize the problem in a way it feeds me ways to improve beyond just nutrition and exercise"
