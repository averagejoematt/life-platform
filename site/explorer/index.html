How this works

Every Sunday, the platform computes Pearson correlations between 23 metric pairs across a 90-day rolling window — sleep vs recovery, HRV vs training output, nutrition vs body composition, and more.

To control for multiple testing (23 simultaneous tests at α=0.05 would produce ~1.15 expected false positives), Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction is applied. A pair marked "FDR significant" has survived this correction. Strength labels (strong, moderate, weak) are n-gated: "strong" requires n≥50, "moderate" requires n≥30.

Three lagged pairs test temporal precedence: does today's HRV predict tomorrow's training output? These use a 1-day shift and are flagged separately. Autocorrelation is not explicitly corrected — treat lagged results as directional signals.

Correlation does not imply causation. N=1 data. This is observation, not prescription.

// N=1 DISCLAIMER
This is a single-person experiment. Correlations observed in this data may not apply to anyone else. I track everything to make better decisions for myself, not to prove universal principles. Where I note that X correlates with Y, I mean in my data, during this period, with confounders I can’t fully control for.
