What Would My Health Board Say?

I've assembled a panel of world-class experts as AI advisors for my transformation. Ask them anything — each responds in their unique voice and with their specific lens.

AI personas inspired by real researchers. Not medical advice.

The world's top performers don't make decisions alone — they have coaches, doctors, nutritionists, and mentors on speed dial. Most people don't have access to that. This is an attempt to change that.

Six expert perspectives are modelled here, each with a distinct domain, a different bias, and genuinely different answers to the same question. Dr. Rhonda Patrick will point you toward micronutrient gaps and cold exposure. Dr. Elena Vasquez will ask about your Zone 2 minutes and metabolic markers. Layne Norton will challenge the evidence base. David Goggins will not care about your excuses. That tension between perspectives is the point — the disagreements are where the insight lives.

// How to ask the right question
  • 01Ask something specific, not general. "Should I eat more protein?" gets a generic answer. "I'm eating 140g of protein daily in a 400-calorie deficit — is that enough to preserve muscle?" gets a useful one.
  • 02Include context about your current state. Mention your goal, relevant recent data, and what you've already tried. The advisors can only reason with what you give them.
  • 03Ask each advisor separately to surface disagreement. Select just one at a time and compare. The gap between how Dr. Vasquez and Goggins answer "should I train today?" reveals more than either answer alone.
  • 04Treat the answers as perspectives, not prescriptions. These are AI personas inspired by real thinkers — not medical advice. Use them to pressure-test your reasoning, not to replace a real doctor.
