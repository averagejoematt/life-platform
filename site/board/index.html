// How to ask the right question
  • 01Ask something specific, not general. "Should I eat more protein?" gets a generic answer. "I'm eating 140g of protein daily in a 400-calorie deficit — is that enough to preserve muscle?" gets a useful one.
  • 02Include context about your current state. Mention your goal, relevant recent data, and what you've already tried. The advisors can only reason with what you give them.
  • 03Ask each advisor separately to surface disagreement. Select just one at a time and compare. The gap between how Dr. Vasquez and Goggins answer "should I train today?" reveals more than either answer alone.
  • 04Treat the answers as perspectives, not prescriptions. These are AI personas inspired by real thinkers — not medical advice. Use them to pressure-test your reasoning, not to replace a real doctor.
