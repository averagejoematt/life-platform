The 80/20

Supplements are roughly 10% of the answer here. The other 90% is reversing whatever changed between May 2025 and April 2026 that drove insulin from 2.5 → 14.3, testosterone from 577 → 361, omega-3 from 7.8% → 3.3%, and lipids significantly worse. The supplement protocol below should run in parallel with — not instead of — addressing food architecture, training, sleep, and the physician conversations.

The true core: creatine, D3, one omega-3, multivitamin, short-term zinc, psyllium, protein support, glycine, retesting. Everything else has to earn its place.

Physician conversation discuss

Conversations the supplement protocol can't substitute for. Not optional — the labs warrant them.

  1. Metformin specifically (name the drug) — Given the insulin trajectory (2.5 → 14.3 µIU/mL), metformin has the strongest evidence base. The prescribing decision is the PCP's — depends on kidney function, full clinical picture, lifestyle interventions underway. But make sure metformin is named specifically; don't let it become a vague "help with my insulin" discussion. Berberine is the fallback if the physician declines.
  2. Statin or PCSK9 inhibitor conversation — ApoB 116, LDL-P 2128, Lp-PLA2 137, hs-CRP 1.4, plus paternal grandmother's heart disease. No supplement on this list will move ApoB by 50 points. This conversation belongs in the next 90 days, not the next 90 weeks.
  3. MASLD/NAFLD imaging — GGT moved 22 → 31 in 12 months alongside rising insulin and worsening lipids. Liver enzymes are still in range, but this is a textbook setup. A FibroScan or liver ultrasound is reasonable.
  4. Absorption workup if 90-day D and omega-3 retests still fail — Stool elastase, GGT trend, possibly a celiac panel. Low-normal amylase + lipase + the failed repletion despite supplementation is a pattern.
  5. Verify the BMI 44.3 figure — Function Health has this on file as a year-over-year change from 28.06. If accurate, it changes the urgency framing significantly. If a data-entry artifact, worth correcting in their system.
  6. Testosterone management — Do NOT chase T-boosters (tongkat ali, fadogia, ashwagandha). Low T is downstream of insulin resistance and adiposity. Treat upstream. Reassess at 6 months; if T is still <450 ng/dL after metabolic improvement, that's the right moment for an endocrinology referral.

Decision rules at retest

If vitamin D <40 ng/mL despite adherence + fat co-ingestion
Escalate to 8000–10000 IU/day AND raise absorption workup with physician.
If omega-3 index still <8%
Verify product quality (oxidation), confirm 2g EPA+DHA dose, consider rule-out for fat malabsorption.
If insulin still >10 µIU/mL
Metformin conversation moves from "should have happened" to immediate. Berberine is no longer optional if PCP still declines.
If ApoB still >100 mg/dL
Statin/PCSK9 conversation moves from "should have" to "now."
If GGT continues climbing
MASLD imaging becomes urgent, not optional.
If hs-CRP still elevated
Look at all inflammation drivers — atopic load, sleep, food, training — and consider conditional curcumin add.
If zinc back in mid-range, copper normal
Step zinc down to multi-only.
If atopic symptoms bothersome
Consider conditional quercetin add.

What "success" looks like in 90 days

  • D ≥40 ng/mL
  • Omega-3 index ≥8%
  • Fasting insulin <8
  • ApoB trending down (even 5–10 points is progress)
  • hs-CRP <1.0
  • GGT stable or trending down
  • Subjective: improved cognition, fewer cravings, better sleep, weight trend reversed
If 4+ of those move in the right direction, the protocol is working. If <2 move, the answer isn't a different supplement stack — it's the lifestyle/physician conversations getting more attention.
