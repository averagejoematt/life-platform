Vitamin D3
5000 IU
Morning Genome-justified
Why I'm taking it

VDR gene variant (Bsm1) reduces receptor binding efficiency, requiring higher-than-standard dosing to maintain adequate serum 25(OH)D levels.

What the science says
  • VDR Bsm1 polymorphism is associated with reduced Vitamin D receptor transcriptional activity, necessitating higher circulating levels for equivalent downstream effect.
  • Serum 25(OH)D above 40 ng/mL is associated with improved immune function, mood regulation, and musculoskeletal health in RCTs.
  • At northern latitudes, endogenous synthesis is insufficient October–April regardless of sun exposure.

Creatine Monohydrate
5g
Morning Peer-reviewed consensus
Why I'm taking it

Most well-studied ergogenic aid in existence — consistent strength and power output gains, with emerging cognitive benefit data.

What the science says
  • Increases phosphocreatine stores in muscle, accelerating ATP resynthesis during high-intensity efforts; meta-analyses show ~8% strength gain over placebo.
  • Creatine supplementation increases brain creatine concentrations and is associated with improved working memory and processing speed in low-meat-intake populations.
  • 5g/day is sufficient for maintenance after a loading phase; no kidney safety concerns in healthy individuals at this dose.

Omega-3 Fish Oil
3g EPA/DHA
Morning Peer-reviewed consensus
Why I'm taking it

FADS2 rs1535 variant impairs conversion of ALA to EPA/DHA, making pre-formed fish oil essential rather than optional for anti-inflammatory benefit.

What the science says
  • EPA and DHA are precursors to resolvins and protectins — pro-resolving lipid mediators that actively terminate inflammatory cascades rather than merely inhibiting initiation.
  • High-dose Omega-3 (2–4g/day EPA+DHA) significantly reduces triglycerides; cardiovascular mortality reduction seen in REDUCE-IT at 4g/day icosapentaenoic acid.
  • FADS2 rs1535 T-allele carriers show substantially lower ALA conversion; pre-formed EPA/DHA is the only reliable dietary source for this genotype.

Tongkat Ali
200mg
Morning N=1 hypothesis
Why I'm taking it

Testing whether Eurycoma longifolia supplementation produces measurable improvement in recovery metrics and WHOOP strain-to-recovery ratio.

What the science says
  • Small RCTs (n=76, 300mg/day for 12 weeks) show statistically significant increases in free testosterone in late-middle-aged men; effect size modest.
  • Proposed mechanism: quassinoids inhibit SHBG binding, increasing bioavailable testosterone fraction rather than stimulating production directly.
  • Evidence base is thin — most trials are industry-funded, small, and short-duration; replication in independent labs is limited.

NMN
500mg
Morning N=1 hypothesis
Why I'm taking it

Testing NAD+ precursor supplementation as a strategy for maintaining mitochondrial function and metabolic flexibility as a longevity lever.

What the science says
  • NAD+ declines ~50% between ages 40 and 60; NMN is efficiently converted to NAD+ in humans (Yoshino et al., 2021 — confirmed bioavailability and blood NAD+ elevation at 250mg/day).
  • Animal studies show substantial metabolic and longevity effects; human RCT data is promising but limited — largest trial (n=80) showed improved muscle insulin sensitivity.
  • Optimal dose, long-term safety profile, and disease-relevant outcomes in humans remain understudied; this is an informed speculative bet.

CoQ10 Ubiquinol
200mg
Morning Peer-reviewed consensus
Why I'm taking it

Mitochondrial electron transport chain cofactor; ubiquinol form selected for superior bioavailability vs. ubiquinone, particularly important given past statin use history.

What the science says
  • Statins inhibit the mevalonate pathway, reducing endogenous CoQ10 synthesis by 40–50%; supplementation at 100–200mg/day restores serum levels to pre-statin baseline.
  • CoQ10 is the mobile electron carrier between Complex I/II and Complex III in the mitochondrial electron transport chain — essential for ATP synthesis efficiency.
  • Meta-analysis of 13 RCTs (Mortensen et al.) shows 200mg ubiquinol produces plasma CoQ10 concentrations approximately twice that of equivalent ubiquinone dose.

Berberine
500mg
With meals N=1 hypothesis
Why I'm taking it

Testing CGM-measurable postprandial glucose attenuation, particularly for high-carbohydrate meals where my response tends to spike above 140 mg/dL.

What the science says
  • AMPK activator — mimics the downstream effects of metformin by increasing insulin sensitivity and reducing hepatic glucose output; several RCTs show HbA1c reduction comparable to metformin in T2D populations.
  • Inhibits intestinal alpha-glucosidase, slowing carbohydrate absorption and blunting postprandial glucose spikes; effect is dose- and timing-dependent (take 20–30 min before eating).
  • Short-term GI discomfort is common at doses above 500mg; bioavailability is low (~5%) but sufficient for AMPK activation at standard doses.

Magnesium Glycinate
400mg
Evening Peer-reviewed consensus
Why I'm taking it

Magnesium is a cofactor in 300+ enzymatic reactions; glycinate chelate selected for superior bioavailability and minimal laxative effect compared to oxide or citrate forms.

What the science says
  • Magnesium is required for NMDA receptor regulation and GABA-ergic signalling — both pathways involved in sleep induction and slow-wave sleep quality.
  • Meta-analysis of 8 RCTs shows magnesium supplementation significantly reduces subjective insomnia severity and improves sleep efficiency in adults with poor sleep quality.
  • Glycinate chelate bypasses intestinal magnesium transporters that become saturated at high doses, achieving higher serum levels with less GI disruption than inorganic salts.

Zinc
30mg
Evening Genome-justified
Why I'm taking it

Zinc metabolism SNP reduces intestinal zinc absorption efficiency, requiring supplementation to maintain adequate serum zinc status for immune and hormonal function.

What the science says
  • Zinc is a cofactor for over 300 enzymes and is essential for testosterone biosynthesis — clinical zinc deficiency is associated with hypogonadism that reverses with supplementation.
  • SLC39A4 (ZIP4) and SLC30A series transporter variants affect zinc homeostasis; impaired absorption variants may double dietary requirement to maintain serum adequacy.
  • Taken in the evening due to competitive absorption with copper (maintained separately at breakfast via diet); evening zinc avoids meal-time mineral interference.

Ashwagandha KSM-66
600mg
Evening Peer-reviewed consensus
Why I'm taking it

Cortisol modulation and stress-resilience support; KSM-66 extract selected as the most clinically studied form with the largest body of human RCT evidence.

What the science says
  • Withania somnifera root extract at 600mg/day (KSM-66) reduces serum cortisol by ~25–28% vs. placebo in chronically stressed adults across multiple double-blind RCTs.
  • Randomized trial (Wankhede et al., 2015) showed significant improvements in muscle recovery, testosterone, and upper/lower body strength vs. placebo over 8 weeks of resistance training.
  • Evening dosing aligns with the goal of attenuating late-day cortisol elevation that impairs sleep onset; half-life is approximately 6–8 hours.

Why Genome-Informed?

Genetic variants affect how you absorb, convert, and utilise nutrients. A 110-SNP analysis identified variants in Matthew's genome that directly influence supplementation decisions.

VDR Bsm1 Reduces Vitamin D receptor binding efficiency — requires 5,000 IU/day to maintain adequate 25(OH)D. Standard advice (600–2,000 IU) is insufficient for this genotype.
FADS2 rs1535 T-allele impairs conversion of ALA to EPA/DHA (delta-6 desaturase activity reduced ~30%). Pre-formed Omega-3 from fish oil is essential — ALA sources (flaxseed, walnuts) are ineffective for this genotype.
SLC39A4 variant ZIP4 transporter variant reduces intestinal zinc absorption — serum zinc runs low without supplementation despite adequate dietary intake. 30mg zinc gluconate restores serum levels to reference range.
