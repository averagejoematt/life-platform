On the methodology

These are N=1 experiments — single-subject self-experiments with no control group. The scientific limitations are real: no blinding, no randomization, seasonal confounders, regression to the mean. Results apply to me specifically and may not generalize.

What makes them useful anyway: consistency of measurement. With 19 live data sources running continuously, the baseline data is dense enough that meaningful signal can emerge from even a single-person study — particularly for outcomes with low day-to-day variance like resting HRV and fasting glucose.

Experiments are tracked inside the Life Platform. Each one has a defined start date, hypothesis variables, and auto-comparison against the pre-experiment window. Results are published here unchanged — including the ones that didn't work.

// N=1 DISCLAIMER
This is a single-person experiment. Correlations observed in this data may not apply to anyone else. I track everything to make better decisions for myself, not to prove universal principles. Where I note that X correlates with Y, I mean in my data, during this period, with confounders I can’t fully control for.
