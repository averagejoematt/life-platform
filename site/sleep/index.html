Sleep is the
highest-leverage input

Every other pillar depends on sleep. HRV recovery, the primary signal Whoop uses to score readiness, is built during deep sleep — specifically during the slow-wave stages that dominate the first half of the night.

Cut sleep short and HRV drops, training adaptations stall, and appetite hormones shift toward hunger. One bad night costs across all seven categories.

That’s why this page cross-references Eight Sleep’s mattress sensor with Whoop’s wrist data to find the conditions that reliably produce quality sleep, not just hours in bed.

01
Target 8 hours in bed — not 7. Buffer for sleep onset and one wake cycle still lands above 7h of actual sleep.
02
Bed temperature 68°F via Eight Sleep pod. Core body temp must drop 1–3°F to initiate deep sleep.
03
No screens 1 hour before bed. Blue light suppresses melatonin onset. Replaced with reading or audio.
04
Consistent 10pm window. Circadian timing matters as much as duration. Irregular schedules fragment slow-wave.
05
Magnesium glycinate before bed (genome-adjusted dose). Supports GABA pathways and muscle relaxation.
Measurement protocol
EIGHT SLEEP POD Mattress-integrated sensor. Active cooling/heating. Sleep score 0–100. Bed temp in °F. Efficiency = total sleep ÷ time in bed.
WHOOP 4.0 Wrist-worn sensor. Sleep stages via actigraphy + optical HRV. Quality score 0–100. Duration measured as actual sleep, not time in bed. Both synced nightly to DynamoDB.
TEMPERATURE ANALYSIS 30-night pod temperature grouped into 2°F buckets. Average sleep score per bucket. Highest-scoring bucket = empirical optimal. N=1 — individual results vary.
