// why it matters

Sleep is the master lever

Every other pillar depends on sleep. HRV recovery, the primary signal Whoop uses to score readiness, is built during deep sleep — specifically during the slow-wave stages that dominate the first half of the night. Cut sleep short and HRV drops, training adaptations stall, and appetite hormones shift toward hunger.

The seven-pillar framework scores sleep as its own domain, but it also flows upstream into every other score: low sleep directly suppresses recovery, impairs glucose regulation, reduces strength output, and degrades stress resilience. One bad night costs across all seven categories.

That's why this page exists. The data doesn't just track sleep duration — it cross-references Eight Sleep's mattress sensor with Whoop's wrist data to find the conditions that reliably produce quality sleep, not just hours in bed.

// Matthew's sleep protocol
01
Target 8 hours in bed — not 7. Buffer for sleep onset and one wake cycle still lands above 7h of actual sleep.
02
Bed temperature 68°F via Eight Sleep pod. Core body temp must drop 1–3°F to initiate deep sleep. Active cooling accelerates that drop.
03
No screens 1 hour before bed. Blue light suppresses melatonin onset. Replaced with reading or audio.
04
Consistent 10pm window. Circadian timing matters as much as duration. Irregular sleep schedules fragment slow-wave regardless of total hours.
05
Magnesium glycinate before bed (genome-adjusted dose). Supports GABA pathways and muscle relaxation without the morning grogginess of sleep aids.

Eight Sleep × Whoop
Two sensors, one picture
Eight Sleep measures from the mattress: pod temperature, sleep stage estimates, respiratory rate, HRV, and toss-and-turns. Whoop measures from the wrist: sleep cycles, disturbances, and recovery impact. Cross-referencing both eliminates sensor bias and surfaces the most reliable sleep signal.
Why temperature matters
The thermoregulation protocol
Core body temperature needs to drop 1–3°F to initiate and maintain deep sleep. The Eight Sleep pod enables precise cooling on a schedule — cooler during the first half of the night (deep sleep phase), slightly warmer in the second half (REM phase). The optimal temperature is individual. This data shows mine.

Measurement Protocol
EIGHT SLEEP POD Mattress-integrated sensor. Continuous tracking via active cooling/heating. Sleep score 0–100. Bed temp in °F. Efficiency = total sleep ÷ time in bed.
WHOOP 4.0 Wrist-worn sensor. Sleep stages via actigraphy + optical HRV. Quality score 0–100. Duration measured as actual sleep, not time in bed. Both systems synced nightly to life-platform DynamoDB.
TEMPERATURE ANALYSIS 30-night pod temperature grouped into 2°F buckets. Average sleep score computed per bucket. Highest-scoring bucket = empirical optimal range. Minimum 7 days required for analysis. N=1 — individual results vary.
