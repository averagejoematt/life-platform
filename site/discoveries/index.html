// Counterintuitive finds

Things that surprised the data

Findings that went against the conventional expectation. This is where the N=1 experiment gets interesting.

// Counterintuitive
More training didn’t immediately improve recovery
Expected: more Zone 2 → higher next-day HRV
Observed: high training load negatively correlated with same-week recovery score when sleep was below target (r≈−0.31)
// Counterintuitive
Sleep quality matters more than sleep duration
Expected: longer sleep → better recovery score
Observed: HRV (quality proxy) correlates more strongly with recovery than total hours. 7h high-HRV sleep > 9h low-HRV sleep.
// Counterintuitive
Consistency beats intensity on weekly habit score
Expected: high-output training days would boost overall weekly score
Observed: the 7 Tier 0 non-negotiable habits predict weekly character score more reliably than any single high-output day.

// N=1 DISCLAIMER
All correlations are Pearson r over a 90-day rolling window on a single person’s data. FDR correction applied. Correlation does not imply causation. These findings changed my behavior — they may not apply to you. This is observation, not prescription.
