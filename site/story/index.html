Chapter 02

The Problem With Previous Attempts

// Matthew — write this section (aim for 2–3 paragraphs)

Start with: "I’d tried to lose weight [how many] times before. The most recent was..."

Questions to answer:
• Name 2–3 specific past attempts (the app, the diet, the plan). What happened?
• How long did each last? What was the pattern — strong start then slow fade?
• What was the *real* reason they failed? (Not "I got busy" — the deeper thing: no feedback loop? No accountability? Lying to yourself about the numbers?)
• The reader should think: "That’s exactly what I do." Write for that person.

Tone: Self-aware, not self-pitying. You’re diagnosing the failure pattern, not wallowing in it.

"The problem wasn't motivation. I had that. The problem was I was making decisions without data."

Chapter 04

What the Data Has Shown

// Matthew — write this section (3–4 paragraphs — the most powerful section)

Start with: "The first thing the data showed me that I didn’t expect was..."

Write about 2–3 specific discoveries:
• Discovery 1 (sleep): What did you learn about your sleep that surprised you? (e.g., Eight Sleep data, sleep architecture, how sleep affects next-day recovery)
• Discovery 2 (nutrition/glucose): What did the CGM or MacroFactor data reveal? A food you thought was fine that spiked glucose? A pattern you never noticed?
• Discovery 3 (the meta-insight): What’s the thing about *tracking itself* that changed your behavior? The feeling of knowing the AI is going to read your data tomorrow morning?

The key: Each discovery should be a story, not a stat. "I learned that when I eat Thai food after 8pm, my HRV drops 15ms the next day" is great. "My HRV improved 12%" is not.

This is where skeptics become believers. The insights need to be personal and surprising enough that readers think “I wonder what MY data would show.”

Chapter 05

Why Public

// Matthew — write this section (2–3 paragraphs)

Start with: "I almost kept this private. The reason I didn’t is..."

Questions to answer:
• What was the moment you decided to make the numbers public? Was there hesitation?
• What does publishing the bad weeks do for you psychologically? (Accountability? Honesty? Proving something?)

• What’s the thing you’re most afraid someone will see in the data?

End with a forward-looking sentence. Where are you going? What does 185 lbs represent beyond a number?
