// who is this

Matthew Walker

Senior Director at a SaaS company in Seattle. IT leadership background. No engineering degree. Just systems thinking, a lot of reading, and Claude as a full-time engineering partner.

I spent 15 years building teams, managing vendors, and running IT infrastructure at scale. My job is making complex systems reliable and getting people to actually use them. I'm not a developer by training — I've never shipped production code at work. But I understand systems, and I understand what it takes to change behavior.

In January 2026, I decided to apply that same thinking to myself. Not a challenge. Not a hack. A proper system — with data sources, intelligence layers, automated coaching, and public accountability.

I built the Life Platform as a solo project: 19 data sources, 95 intelligence tools, 48 Lambda functions, and an AI that writes me a coaching brief every morning. The whole thing runs on AWS for under $15 a month. Every number is real. Every failure is included.

This project also became a proof of concept for something I care about professionally: what happens when non-engineers get genuine access to AI development tools? My team is responsible for rolling out Claude across my company. I'm living the experiment I'm trying to get other people to believe in.

Technical context

The build

Role Solo developer. Claude handles all code generation; I handle architecture, product decisions, and every deploy.
Infrastructure AWS — Lambda, DynamoDB, S3, CloudFront, EventBridge, SES, Secrets Manager. Single AWS account, us-west-2.
Intelligence Anthropic Claude via MCP protocol — 95 tools exposed to Claude for live data access. Opus 4 for clinical reasoning, Sonnet 4 for structured outputs, Haiku 4 for lightweight daily compute.
Data sources 19 active — Whoop, Eight Sleep, Withings, Garmin, Strava, Apple Health, MacroFactor, Habitify, Notion, Todoist, Dexcom Stelo, genome, labs, DEXA, weather, travel, blood pressure, supplements, state of mind.
Test coverage 83/83 tests passing. Architecture Review #16: grade A. CI/CD pipeline with auto-rollback.
Cost ~$13/month on AWS. Anthropic API calls are the variable cost; Lambda compute is negligible.
Purpose Personal health intelligence, accountability, and a live demonstration of what non-engineers can build with AI. Started January 2026.
