Training for the
centenarian decathlon

Most programs optimize for aesthetics at 30. This one optimizes for carrying groceries, climbing stairs, and getting off the floor at 85.

You lose 8–12% of strength per decade after 40. To deadlift your bodyweight at 85, you need ~2x bodyweight now. Four data sources — Strava, Hevy, Garmin, Whoop — feed a single training load model that cross-references with nutrition and sleep data.

01
150+ min/week Zone 2 — walking, cycling, rucking. HR 114–133. Highest-evidence longevity modality.
02
3x/week compound lifts — squat, deadlift, bench, OHP. Progressive overload toward centenarian benchmarks.
03
ACWR <1.5 — Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio guards against injury spikes.
04
Recovery-gated intensity — Whoop <34% triggers deload suggestion in daily brief.
05
80/20 polarization — 80% low intensity, 20% high. No Zone 3 junk miles.
Measurement protocol
STRAVAAll cardio: runs, rides, walks, rucks. GPS + HR from Garmin. Duration, distance, avg HR synced daily.
HEVYAll strength: sets, reps, weight. 1RM via Brzycki. Volume load = sets × reps × weight.
ZONE 2HR 60–70% max (114–133 bpm). Target: 150 min/week (WHO + Attia). N=1 thresholds.
