Experiments
vs
Challenges

Experiments are science. They have a falsifiable hypothesis, a controlled protocol, a minimum duration, and published data. They test whether something works. They belong in the Lab.

Challenges are action. No hypothesis required. Pick a protocol, commit to the duration, see what happens. They test whether you can do it. They belong in the Arena.

Challenges can graduate into experiments — if a 7-day step challenge reveals that 10,000 steps correlates with better sleep, that becomes a formal hypothesis. And experiments can spawn challenges — if a completed experiment proves cold showers boost morning HRV, that becomes a recurring challenge for anyone to try.

Where do challenges come from? Five sources: journal mining (your own words surface avoidance patterns), data signals (weak pillars, broken streaks), hypothesis graduates (confirmed patterns worth testing behaviourally), science scans (latest research), and manual creation (you or the community).
