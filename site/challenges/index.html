Experiments are science. They have a falsifiable hypothesis, a controlled protocol, a minimum duration, and published data. They test whether something works. They belong in the Lab.

Challenges are action. No hypothesis required. Pick a protocol, commit to the duration, see what happens. They test whether you can do it. They belong in the Arena.

Challenges can graduate into experiments — if a 7-day step challenge reveals that 10,000 steps correlates with better sleep, that becomes a formal hypothesis. Experiments can spawn challenges — if cold showers boost morning HRV, that becomes a recurring challenge for anyone to try.

Where do challenges come from? Five sources: journal mining, data signals, hypothesis graduates, science scans, and board recommendations. Each challenge shows its source and which board member recommended it.
