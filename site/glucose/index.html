288 readings per day
vs one fasting number

A fasting glucose test gives you one snapshot taken after 8+ hours without food. It captures none of the metabolic action: not what happens after meals, not whether glucose spikes at 2am, not whether a salad or a steak drives different post-meal curves in your body specifically.

A continuous glucose monitor reads glucose every 5 minutes, 24 hours a day. The additional signal includes glucose variability, post-meal spike magnitude, nocturnal patterns, and how specific foods interact with your individual physiology — not population averages.

Time-in-range is now considered a more actionable metric than HbA1c because it responds to intervention within days, not months.

01
Dexcom Stelo CGM — worn continuously, no prescription required. Replaced every 15 days. Syncs to life-platform automatically.
02
Target TIR >90% in the 70–180 mg/dL range and >70% in the tighter 70–120 optimal window.
03
Protein-first meals moved the needle most. Eating protein and fat before carbs blunts the spike by slowing gastric emptying.
04
10-minute walk after eating consistently drops post-meal peak. Muscle contraction creates glucose uptake independent of insulin.
05
Reducing refined carbs eliminated the majority of >140 readings. Whole-food carbs with fiber produce much smaller spikes.
Measurement protocol
SENSOR Dexcom Stelo OTC CGM. 15-day wear cycle. 5-minute reading interval. No calibration required. 288 data points per day.
TIME IN RANGE (TIR) Standard: 70–180 mg/dL (ADA guideline). Optimal: 70–120 mg/dL (longevity medicine target). Target: >90% standard, >70% optimal.
VARIABILITY (SD) Standard deviation of all 5-min readings in a 24-hour period. SD <15: excellent. 15–20: good. >20: elevated metabolic stress. N=1 data — individual baselines vary.
