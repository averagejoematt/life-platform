Why variability matters
Glucose variability
Standard deviation (SD) measures how much glucose swings day-to-day. Even with a normal average, high variability indicates metabolic stress — large spikes and crashes that don't show up in HbA1c. Target: SD < 20 mg/dL (Attia / Levels protocol). Lower is better. Stable glucose = stable energy, mood, and cognition.
Data source
Dexcom Stelo CGM
Continuous glucose monitoring via Dexcom Stelo — a reading every 5 minutes, 288 data points per day. Synced to Apple Health, ingested nightly into life-platform DynamoDB. Daily aggregates computed: avg, min, max, SD, time-in-range (70–180 mg/dL), and Attia optimal range (70–120 mg/dL).
Measurement Protocol
SENSOR Dexcom Stelo OTC CGM. 15-day wear cycle. 5-minute reading interval. No calibration required.
TIME IN RANGE (TIR) Standard: 70–180 mg/dL (ADA guideline for non-diabetic). Optimal: 70–120 mg/dL (Attia longevity target, stricter). Target: >90% standard, >70% optimal.
VARIABILITY (SD) Standard deviation of all 5-min readings in a 24-hour period. SD <15: excellent. 15–20: good. >20: elevated metabolic stress. N=1 data — individual baselines vary.
